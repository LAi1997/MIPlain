First, here are some basics of the game. The Island game is a two-players game.
The two players, green and orange, take turns conquering territories on the world below.

You can conquer any untaken territory until the game has not terminated.
Conquering a territory gives you one or several resources. For instance, conquering the top left territory would give you one Castle and one River.
Each resource belongs to one of the following category:
Island 1, Island 2, Island 3, Forest, Castle, Cornfield, River, Animal.

A TRIPLET is a collection of three resources of the same category. The player who first succeeds in obtaining a triplet of one category of resource wins the game. One player has a triplet in each the following board states, the territories containing the resources of the triplet are highlighted.



A PAIR is a collection of two resources of the same category for one player, the third resource not being taken yet. One player has one or two pairs in each the following board states, the territories containing the resources of the pairs are highlighted.
